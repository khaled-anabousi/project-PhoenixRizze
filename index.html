What are Feasibility Studies?

A Feasibility study is a comprehensive analysis conducted before implementing any investment project to ensure its economic and commercial viability, determine chances of success or failure, estimate expected costs and returns, and assess risks and opportunities. It is an essential step for any successful project, regardless of its type or size.

A feasibility study typically consists of several key elements, starting with a description of the project idea and its objectives, clarifying the products or services the project will offer, and what distinguishes them from competitors. It also includes market research and competitive analysis, studying demand and supply in the target market, analysing customer behaviour, and examining competitors' strengths and weaknesses, in addition to identifying expected opportunities and threats.

The study also addresses technical aspects of the project, such as selecting the appropriate location, determining the project's needs for equipment, human resources, and required technologies, while estimating technical and operational costs. Among the important aspects is the financial study, which includes estimating investment and operational costs, expected revenues, break-even analysis, capital recovery period, and return on investment. Finally, results and recommendations are summarised in a final report that helps decision-makers choose the best ways to implement the project or avoid potential risks.

Preparing a professional feasibility study is the first and fundamental step to ensure the success and sustainability of any investment project.
